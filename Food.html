Batata mash is a kind of potato mash prepared using a variety of sweet potato called batata. This dish is particularly prepared with boiled and mashed batatas. They are mixed with butter and unsweetened coconut and made into a creamy mash. Batata mash is usually eaten as a side dish.
Te inai, also known as fried parrotfish, is a dish made from inai, a colorful reef fish common in Kiribati. Typically served whole, the fish is first marinated in a combination of lemon juice, garlic, and seasonings before being deep-fried in hot oil until it is crispy.
